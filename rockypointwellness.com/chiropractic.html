Chiropractic

Chiropractic comes from the Greek words “chiro” and “praxis” meaning: practice or treatment by hand. Chiropractors are primary healthcare providers who diagnose, manage, and prevent conditions involving muscles, joints and the nervous system. They are musculoskeletal specialists who seek to address the underlying cause of dysfunction or pain.

Chiropractors treat:
  • back pain
  • neck pain
  • headaches
  • sports injuries
  • muscle strains
  • joint injuries
  • arthritis
  • whiplash injuries
  • extremity injuries
  • and more!

Common Questions

What services and therapies can a chiropractor offer?
The staple of Chiropractic care is the adjustment. Chiropractors are specialists in manually adjusting joints of the spine and other joints of the body. However, Chiropractors are also trained in various soft-tissue therapies, rehabilitative exercises, nutritional counselling, various modalities, injury prevention, and lifestyle education. These treatments are used to relieve pain and improve function.

What is an adjustment (spinal manipulative therapy)?
A Chiropractic adjustment or spinal manipulative therapy is a highly-skilled and precise movement directed to the spine, which corrects joint motion, restores movement, and improves function. When a joint is adjusted, an air bubble may escape from the capsule resulting in a popping noise, similar to what happens when you crack your knuckles.

What are some benefits of Chiropractic care?
Chiropractic care can lead to improved movement, better posture, relief from headaches, back and neck pain, alleviation of pregnancy-related pain, prevention of work-related injuries, enhanced athletic performance, improvement flexibility, correction of gait problems and more.

Why is back health so important?
The spine protects the nervous system, which carries important messages throughout the body and interconnects body systems! Physical and emotional stress, which may be the result of repetitive activities at work, lifestyle, accidents or injuries, can result in pressure on nerves that exit the spine by the vertebrae. This is why Chiropractors will assess and treat the back even when symptoms are in the arms or legs.
